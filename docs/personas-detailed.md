### Background

Maria lives with her husband and two children in a subdivision in Lucena. She commutes to work in the city proper daily. She's generally prepared - keeps emergency numbers on the fridge - but admits she can never remember which number is for what.

### Goals

- Keep her family safe at all times
- Get help quickly during any emergency
- Know exactly when help will arrive
- Stay informed about what's happening

### Frustrations

- Can't memorize all emergency numbers
- Gets confused about which number to call
- Doesn't know if help is really coming after she makes a call
- Her mother in the province has no idea what numbers to call

### Scenario - The Day Everything Went Wrong

> It's 6:30 AM. Maria is preparing breakfast when she hears a loud crash from the street. She runs outside and sees a motorcycle lying on the road and a man lying motionless nearby. A car hit the motorcyclist and sped away.
>
> **6:31 AM** - Maria grabs her phone. Her hands are shaking. She thinks "Police? Ambulance? What number?" She knows there's 911 but she's not sure it works in Quezon. She scrolls through her contacts but can't find any emergency numbers saved.
>
> **6:33 AM** - She calls her husband. He doesn't answer - he's in a meeting. She calls her brother. He says he'll try to find the number.
>
> **6:38 AM** - Her brother calls back with a number but says it's for the municipal hall, not sure if they handle emergencies. By now, a small crowd has gathered but no one knows what to do.
>
> **6:45 AM** - Someone finally calls 117 (PNP). The police say they'll send someone but can't give an ETA. Another person calls what they think is an ambulance number.
>
> **7:15 AM** - Police arrive. The ambulance never came. A passing tricycle driver offers to take the injured man to the hospital.
>
> The motorcyclist survived but suffered serious injuries. Doctors said if help arrived sooner, the outcome could have been better.

### How This Product Helps Maria

- One tap to report - app auto-categorizes and routes
- Sees on map exactly where the responder is
- Gets push notifications: "Police dispatched," "En route," "Arrived"
- Can send photos of the scene
- Can chat with responder for more details

### Background

Kapitan Boter has been barangay captain for 8 years. He's responsible for 15,000 residents in his barangay. He needs to coordinate emergency response, disaster management, and communicate with residents quickly.

### Goals

- Keep his barangay safe
- Communicate quickly with all residents during emergencies
- Coordinate with police, fire, and ambulance
- Track incidents in his barangay

### Frustrations

- No fast way to alert all residents
- Hard to know what's happening in real-time
- Limited communication with higher agencies
- Paper-based reporting is slow

### Scenario - The Flash Flood

> It's 3:00 AM. Heavy rains cause flash flooding in low-lying areas of the barangay. Kapitan Boter gets a call from a tanod that water is rising fast.
>
> **3:05 AM** - He opens the app and sends a mass alert: "FLOOD WARNING - Evacuate to higher ground immediately"
>
> **3:06 AM** - 3,000 residents receive the alert on their phones
>
> **3:07 AM** - Residents start evacuating. Some respond that they're safe, others need help.
>
> **3:15 AM** - Kapitan Boter sees on his dashboard: 500 evacuated, 50 need rescue, 10 injured.
>
> **3:20 AM** - He coordinates rescue for those trapped and dispatches to injured.
>
> **5:00 AM** - All residents safe. He generates a report for the city.

### How This Product Helps Kapitan Boter

- **Mass notification**: Alert all residents in seconds
- **Location-based alerts**: Alert only those in danger zones
- **Real-time dashboard**: See all incidents in barangay
- **Multi-agency coordination**: Police, fire, ambulance work together
- **Incident reports**: Auto-generate reports for accountability
