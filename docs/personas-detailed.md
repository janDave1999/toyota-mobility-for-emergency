## Persona 1: Maria Santos

**Age:** 35 years old  
**Role:** Citizen / Emergency Witness
**Location:** Lucena City, Quezon Province

### Demographics

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Location** | Lucena City, Quezon Province |
| **Demographics** | Married, 2 children (8 and 12), works as office administrator |
| **Income** | PHP 25,000/month |
| **Education** | College graduate |
| **Tech Proficiency** | Average - uses Facebook, Viber, GCash regularly |
| **Phone** | Android (Samsung A14), data plan PHP 300/month |

### Background

Maria lives with her husband and two children in a subdivision in Lucena. She commutes to work in the city proper daily. She's generally prepared - keeps emergency numbers on the fridge - but admits she can never remember which number is for what.

### Goals

- Keep her family safe at all times
- Get help quickly during any emergency
- Know exactly when help will arrive
- Stay informed about what's happening

### Frustrations

- Can't memorize all emergency numbers
- Gets confused about which number to call
- Doesn't know if help is really coming after she makes a call
- Her mother in the province has no idea what numbers to call

### Scenario - The Day Everything Went Wrong

> It's 6:30 AM. Maria is preparing breakfast when she hears a loud crash from the street. She runs outside and sees a motorcycle lying on the road and a man lying motionless nearby. A car hit the motorcyclist and sped away.
>
> **6:31 AM** - Maria grabs her phone. Her hands are shaking. She thinks "Police? Ambulance? What number?" She knows there's 911 but she's not sure it works in Quezon. She scrolls through her contacts but can't find any emergency numbers saved.
>
> **6:33 AM** - She calls her husband. He doesn't answer - he's in a meeting. She calls her brother. He says he'll try to find the number.
>
> **6:38 AM** - Her brother calls back with a number but says it's for the municipal hall, not sure if they handle emergencies. By now, a small crowd has gathered but no one knows what to do.
>
> **6:45 AM** - Someone finally calls 117 (PNP). The police say they'll send someone but can't give an ETA. Another person calls what they think is an ambulance number.
>
> **7:15 AM** - Police arrive. The ambulance never came. A passing tricycle driver offers to take the injured man to the hospital.
>
> The motorcyclist survived but suffered serious injuries. Doctors said if help arrived sooner, the outcome could have been better.

### How This Product Helps Maria

- One tap to report - app auto-categorizes and routes
- Sees on map exactly where the responder is
- Gets push notifications: "Police dispatched," "En route," "Arrived"
- Can send photos of the scene
- Can chat with responder for more details

### Background

Juan has been patrolling Cebu City streets for 15 years. He's seen every kind of emergency - from petty crimes to murders. He prides himself on fast response but often frustrated by outdated systems.

### Goals

- Respond to emergencies as fast as possible
- Get accurate location information
- Communicate with victims/witnesses
- Coordinate with other units efficiently
- Go home safe to his family

### Frustrations

- Dispatch gives vague locations ("near the church in Basilica")
- Can't contact the caller once dispatched
- Manual radio communication takes too long
- No way to see other units' locations
- Paper-based logbooks still used

### Scenario - The Chase

> It's 2:00 AM. Sgt. Juan is on night patrol near Colon Street when the dispatch radio crackles: "All units, robbery in progress at a convenience store near City Hall."
>
> **2:01 AM** - Juan acknowledges and asks for more details. Dispatch says they only have a phone call, no details on suspect description or direction of escape.
>
> **2:03 AM** - Juan drives toward City Hall but doesn't know which convenience store. There are 3 within a kilometer. He asks dispatch to call back the reporter but the line is busy.
>
> **2:08 AM** - Juan arrives at the first convenience store - nothing happening. He tries the second - also normal. The third, he finds the store broken into but the suspects are gone.
>
> **2:15 AM** - Dispatch finally reaches the reporter who says it was actually near the public market, not City Hall. Juan drives there but it's been 15 minutes. Suspects long gone.
>
> Later, Juan learns the reporter tried calling back but couldn't get through to dispatch.

### How This Product Helps Sgt. Juan

- Gets alert with exact GPS location on his phone
- Sees the incident on map with reporter's photo/upload
- Can call or chat directly with reporter
- Sees other units' locations to coordinate
- Updates status with one tap: "En route," "On scene," "Incident resolved"

### Goals

- Monitor all active incidents in real-time
- Ensure every emergency has a responder en route

- Support barangay tanods who may not have individual apps
- Maintain accurate logs for accountability

### Frustrations (Current System)

- Multiple phone lines ringing simultaneously
- Callers give vague locations ("near the mall")
- No way to verify if report is real or prank
- Can't track where trucks are once dispatched
- Paper logs and manual radio communication

### Scenario - The Big One (With New System)

> It's 5:00 PM on a dry summer afternoon. Three major fires reported within 30 minutes. But now, Elena monitors a screen where she can see:
>
> **5:02 PM** - New fire report: Apartment in Tondo. System shows: **Engine 12 self-dispatched, en route**. Elena monitors.
>
> **5:05 PM** - New fire: Warehouse in Navotas. System shows: **Engine 8 self-dispatched, en route**. Elena monitors.
>
> **5:08 PM** - New fire: School in Sta. Ana. System shows: **No responders yet in range**.
>
> **5:09 PM** - Elena sees no one responded. She taps "Assign Nearest" - Engine 7 (Station 3) receives alert and accepts.
>
> **5:15 PM** - Elena monitors all three fires progressing. All responders tracked in real-time on her dashboard.
>
> **5:45 PM** - All fires under control. Elena generates incident report with one click.

### How This Product Helps Elena

- **Monitors** - sees all emergencies on one map
- **Supports** - can assign units only when no one responds
- **Tracks** - real-time location of all trucks visible
- **Coordinates** - during mass emergencies/disasters
- **Logs** - status updates auto-logged for accountability
- **Support role** - not overwhelmed since responders self-dispatch

- Receives **automatic push notifications** when medical emergency happens within 500m - no mode switching needed
- Can accept/decline to respond
- Shows her location to the ambulance
- Can communicate with arriving EMTs
- Her credentials verified through PRC database

> It's 2:00 AM. Junjun is doing his rounds near the market area when he hears gunshots. Three shots. Then screaming.
>
> **2:02 AM** - Junjun runs toward the sound. He sees two men on motorcycles, one holding a gun. They're robbing a sari-sari store. The store owner is on the ground, bleeding.
>
> **2:03 AM** - Junjun tries to call for backup on his radio. Static. He tries his phone but the signal is weak in the area.
>
> **2:05 AM** - The robbers see Junjun approaching. They finish grabbing what they can and speed away on their motorcycles. Junjun is alone - he has a baton but they're armed and already gone.
>
> **2:06 AM** - Junjun runs to check on the store owner. The man is badly injured but alive. Junjun tries again to call for help - still no signal.
>
> **2:15 AM** - Junjun runs to the main road where there's better signal. He finally gets through to the barangay captain. The captain says he'll call PNP. By the time police arrive, the robbers are long gone.
>
> **2:45 AM** - PNP arrives, takes statements. Junjun feels helpless - he was there but couldn't call for backup in time.
>
> "Kung may magandang communication device ako, kaya ko silang hulihin" (If I had better communication, I could have caught them).

### How This Product Helps Junjun

- GPS-tracked so dispatch knows exactly where he is
- One-tap panic button to alert other tanods and PNP
- Sees all active incidents in his barangay on a map
- Can call for backup instantly
- Incident details including photos/videos from witnesses
